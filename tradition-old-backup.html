Tradition Map

A structured overview of the philosophical traditions surrounding Plato and Aristotle — from their predecessors, through their schools, into late antiquity, the medieval world, and modern philosophy.

Main Lineage

From Socrates to late antiquity

Pre-Socratics — Thales, Parmenides, Heraclitus, Pythagoreans
Socrates (469–399 BC) — ethical inquiry, elenchus, examined life
Plato (427–347 BC) — Academy, dialogues, Forms, Republic, Symposium
Aristotle (384–322 BC) — Lyceum, treatises, categories, logic, ethics, politics
The Academy (Plato's School)
Ancient Platonism
  • Speusippus — Plato's nephew and successor at the Academy.
  • Xenocrates — Develops Platonic metaphysics and theology.
  • Arcesilaus — Turns the Academy toward skepticism; suspension of judgment.
  • Carneades — New Academy; sophisticated skeptical critiques of Stoic dogmatism.
The Lyceum (Aristotle's School)
Peripatetic tradition
  • Theophrastus — Aristotle's successor; expands botanical and ethical studies.
  • Strato of Lampsacus — Emphasizes natural science; more mechanistic leanings.
  • Later Peripatetics — Andronicus of Rhodes edits and arranges Aristotle's corpus.

Branches & Reception

Hellenistic, Medieval, and Modern

Hellenistic Schools
4th – 1st c. BC
  • Stoics (Zeno, Chrysippus) — Adopt Socratic ethics, dialogue with Plato/Aristotle.
  • Epicureans (Epicurus, Lucretius) — Counter teleology with atomism and hedonism.
Late Antique Platonism
3rd – 6th c. AD
  • Plotinus — Neoplatonism; grand metaphysical system around the One.
  • Porphyry — Introduces Aristotle through the Isagoge.
  • Proclus — Systematic commentator; synthesizes Platonism into theology.
  • Simplicius — Preserves Greek philosophy within a Neoplatonic framework.
Medieval Traditions
Latin, Islamic, Jewish
  • Augustine — Christian Platonist; reads Plato into Christian theology.
  • Avicenna — Islamic Aristotelian; essence-existence distinction.
  • Averroes — The great Commentator on Aristotle.
  • Maimonides — Jewish Aristotelian; blends metaphysics with biblical religion.
  • Thomas Aquinas — Synthesizes Aristotle with Christian doctrine.
Modern Era
17th – 20th c.
  • Descartes — Breaks from scholastic physics; revives Platonic rationalism.
  • Kant — Reworks Aristotle's categories into a critical philosophy.
  • Hegel — Reads history of philosophy as unfolding of Spirit.
  • Nietzsche — Radical critique of Platonism and its otherworldly values.
  • Heidegger — Re-reads Greek concepts of physis, being, and truth.
